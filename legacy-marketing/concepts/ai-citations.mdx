When someone asks ChatGPT for the best project management tool, or asks Perplexity to recommend an SEO platform, the brands named in those answers are receiving **AI citations**. An AI citation occurs any time an AI engine — ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Perplexity, or Copilot — mentions, recommends, or links to your brand or content in a response. If your brand doesn't appear in those answers, you're invisible to a growing share of your potential audience before they ever open a browser tab.

## Why AI Citations Matter

Search didn't die. It split in two. Roughly half of your potential audience still Googles and clicks blue links — and the other half types their questions directly into an AI engine and acts on whatever answer comes back. Traditional SEO optimizes for the first half. AI citations determine whether you exist for the second.

The consequences are compounding. When an AI engine consistently cites a competitor in your category, that competitor earns credibility, clicks, and conversions from every person who asks that question — without you ever knowing a race was happening. Tracking AI citations turns an invisible contest into a measurable one you can actually win.

## How Spyro Measures Citations

Spyro runs daily citation audits across all five major AI engines. For each tracked keyword or topic, Spyro queries the AI engines and records whether — and how prominently — your brand appears in the response.

<Note>
  Citation results are reported as **frequencies, not binary yes/no facts**. Because AI engines generate different responses on each run, no single query can definitively confirm whether you're cited or not. Spyro runs multiple queries per topic and surfaces how often your brand appears across those runs — giving you a statistically meaningful signal rather than a snapshot that might flip the next time the model is queried.
</Note>

Your citation data refreshes every 24 hours. If you've just completed setup, your first results will appear within 24 hours.

## The Five AI Engines Spyro Monitors

Spyro tracks citations across the platforms that collectively handle the vast majority of AI-driven queries today:

| AI Engine | Model | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|
| **ChatGPT** | GPT-4o | Largest consumer AI user base; heavily used for product and service recommendations |
| **Claude** | Claude 3 (Anthropic) | Growing enterprise adoption; favors well-sourced, authoritative content |
| **Gemini** | Gemini (Google) | Deeply integrated with Google Search and Google Workspace |
| **Perplexity** | Multiple models | Answer-first search engine; citations are prominently displayed to users |
| **Copilot** | GPT-4o (Microsoft) | Built into Windows, Edge, and Microsoft 365; massive passive reach |

## What the Citation Data Shows You

Once Spyro has gathered your citation data, you'll see:

- **Your citation frequency** — how often your brand appears across AI engine responses for each tracked topic
- **Competitor citation data** — which competing brands are being cited instead of you, and how frequently
- **Topic coverage gaps** — which questions and topics the AI engines answer without citing anyone in your current content footprint, representing open opportunities

This data feeds directly into Spyro's audit and content creation workflow, so every gap you discover becomes an actionable item rather than just a data point.
