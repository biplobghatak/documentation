Setting up Spyro takes just a few minutes. You create an account, tell Spyro
your website address, and Spyro takes a quick look at your site to learn your
brand, your audience, and your competitors. When you're done, you land on your
**dashboard** - your home base for everything else.

A **workspace** is simply one website you want to grow. Everything you do in
Spyro - audits, rankings, content, and settings - lives inside a workspace.
Setup creates your first one.

## Why this matters

The few questions Spyro asks during setup help it work smarter for you later.
Once Spyro knows your brand, who you sell to, and who you compete with, it can
write better content, ask the right questions to AI assistants, and give you
fixes that actually fit your site.

## Sign up

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/sign_in.avif" alt="Spyro sign-in screen" />
</Frame>

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create your account">
    Open the sign-up page and start your free trial. Enter your name, email, and
    a password - or choose **Continue with Google** to skip the password step.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm your email">
    Spyro emails you a short verification code. Type it in to confirm your
    address. If it doesn't arrive, click **Resend code**. (If you signed up with
    Google, you can skip this step.)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add your website">
    On the first setup screen, enter your website address (for example,
    `example.com`) and choose **Analyze**. Spyro starts learning about your site
    right away.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
Already have an account? Just use the **Log in** page instead, with your email
and password (or **Continue with Google**).
</Tip>

## What Spyro does automatically

The moment you add your website, Spyro takes a quick look at it and pre-fills
much of your setup for you. Anything it figures out shows up with an
**Auto-detected from your website** label, so you know it came from your site.

Spyro automatically works out:

- Your **brand name, brand color, logo, and language**
- Your **audience** - who your site is for, and a short description of your
  business
- Your **competitors** - other sites going after the same customers
- A starter set of **AI-visibility questions** - the buyer questions Spyro will
  ask AI assistants to see if they mention you

<Note>
Spyro only reads your **public** pages - the same ones any visitor can see.
Everything it fills in is a **draft you can edit**. Change anything you like, and
Spyro won't overwrite your edits.
</Note>

## Finish the setup

Spyro walks you through a short, friendly wizard so you can review and tweak what
it found. You can move forward and back, or jump to any step:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Business profile" icon="building">
    Confirm the basics - brand name, color, logo, language, and country. Your
    country helps Spyro find the right competitors for your region.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Your audience" icon="users">
    Review who your site is for and your business description. Keep at least one
    audience group to continue.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Competitors" icon="crosshairs">
    Review the rivals Spyro found. Add or remove any. If the list looks off,
    click **Retry** to search again.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Blog setup" icon="newspaper">
    Point Spyro at your existing content to learn from - your blog and a few of
    your best articles. You can also connect Google Search Console here to bring
    in real search data.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Article style" icon="pen-nib">
    Set the defaults for content Spyro writes for you - tone, focus, publishing
    pace, and featured-image style. You can change these later.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="AI visibility questions" icon="robot">
    Review the buyer questions Spyro will ask AI assistants every week. Edit them
    to match how your customers really search for what you offer.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="A couple of quick questions" icon="comment">
    Spyro asks whether you'd like a hand setting up, and how you found Spyro.
    Then choose **Finish setup**.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Landing in your workspace

When you choose **Finish setup**, Spyro takes you to your dashboard and starts
the heavy lifting in the background - looking over your whole site, running your
first audit, and putting together a starter content plan.

You'll see a setup banner and a content-plan progress note while that work runs.
The cards on your dashboard fill in on their own as the results come in - no need
to refresh.

<Tip>
If you close the tab partway through, just reopen Spyro. It picks up right where
you left off, and finished workspaces drop you straight on the dashboard.
</Tip>
