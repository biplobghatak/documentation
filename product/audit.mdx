The **Site audit** crawls your whole site and scores it on two axes - classic
**SEO** (how well search engines can find, read and rank your pages) and **GEO**
(how citable your content is to AI engines). It returns a scored report, groups
every issue by severity, checks whether AI crawlers can reach you, and lets you
drill into any single page. You'll find it under **SEO → Site audit** at
`/{org}/{workspace}/audit`.

<Note>
This is the **product guide** - how to use the audit screen. For how the crawler
and scoring are built, see [Audit](/backend/audit) in the developer docs.
</Note>

## Run an audit

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the audit screen">
    Go to **Site audit** in the sidebar. If you've never audited this workspace,
    you'll see a **Run audit** button.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Start the crawl">
    Click **Run audit**. Spyro queues a full-site crawl. The button switches to
    **Crawling…** with a spinner, and the page refreshes itself every few seconds
    until the audit finishes - you don't need to reload.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Read the report">
    When the crawl completes, the page fills in: a summary header with your scores,
    the four audit views, and a downloadable PDF. Re-run it any time to refresh the
    scores and see what changed.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
You don't have to re-crawl the whole site to test a fix. Use **URL Checks** (below)
to re-check a single URL on demand, or the **Re-check after fixes** button on any
individual page report.
</Tip>

## The summary header

Whatever view you're on, the top of the page is an always-on summary:

- Two large gauge rings - **Site Health** (your SEO score) and **AI Search
  Health** (your GEO score), each 0-100.
- **Severity stat cards** - counts of **Errors**, **Warnings** and **Notices**
  across the whole site.
- **Thematic score cards** - one mini-gauge per audit theme (Technical SEO,
  Performance, Security, Mobile, Advanced SEO, AI Visibility), each with a
  **View details** link that opens the full report pre-filtered to that theme.

### Score bands

Both scores share the same 0-100 scale and the same labels:

| Score | Band |
| --- | --- |
| 85-100 | **Excellent** |
| 70-84 | **Good** |
| 50-69 | **Needs work** |
| 0-49 | **Poor** |

Per-page scores use the same thresholds, shown as colored pills (green for
excellent down to red for poor).

## The views

The nav bar under the summary has four views. The default is **Pages**; the others
add a `?view=` parameter to the URL.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Pages - ?view=pages (default)" icon="layout-list">
    Every crawled page, lowest-scoring first. Each row shows the page's **SEO
    score**, **GEO score**, a worst-severity badge (**Critical** / **Warning** /
    **Notice** / **Healthy**), HTTP status and word count. Click any row to open
    its [per-page drill-down](#the-per-page-drill-down).
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Full report - ?view=report" icon="file-text">
    Every issue found, grouped into **Errors**, **Warnings** and **Notices**, each
    section sorted by how many pages are affected. Each issue lists the affected
    URLs and a **How to fix** note. You can filter the report to one theme - for
    example `?view=report&filter=technical_seo` - which is what the summary's
    **View details** links do.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="History - ?view=history" icon="trending-up">
    Your audit trend over time. A line chart plots **Site Health** and **AI
    Visibility** across past audits, a **What changed since last crawl** panel
    lists newly detected vs. resolved issues, and a **score forecast** estimates
    where your score could go (see below). The chart needs at least two audits -
    with only one, it prompts you to run another to unlock the trend.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="URL Checks - ?view=url-checks" icon="list-checks">
    On-demand checks of individual URLs, outside the full-site crawl. Add a URL,
    click **Add & check**, and Spyro scores just that page (refreshing every few
    seconds until done). Each tracked URL keeps its own SEO/GEO scores and issue
    history - handy for spot-checking a single page after edits.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

### The score forecast

On the **History** view, the **score forecast** bar projects three states side by
side: your **Current** Site Health, the score **After quick wins** (fixing all
notice-level issues, with the point gain and an effort estimate in minutes), and
your **Max potential** if every SEO issue were fixed. A segmented bar visualises
the gap between them.

## Issues by severity

Every issue is one of three severities, and pages roll up to their worst:

| Severity | Shown as | Page badge |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `critical` | **Error** (red) | **Critical** |
| `warning` | **Warning** (amber) | **Warning** |
| `info` | **Notice** (blue) | **Notice** |
| _(none)_ | - | **Healthy** |

## AI-bot access and GEO

GEO scoring is about being *citable* by AI engines - and that starts with letting
their crawlers in.

- **AI crawler access (site-wide)** - a table of known AI bots, each marked
  **Allowed** or **Blocked** based on your `robots.txt`. A blocked bot can't read
  or cite your content.
- **Citation-readiness checklist** - 16 checks that each **Pass** or **Fail**,
  covering things like an answer-first opening (BLUF), question-style headings,
  FAQ / HowTo / Article schema, statistics and figures, an author byline and
  E-E-A-T signals, an `llms.txt` file, a visible freshness date, content that's
  extractable without JavaScript, and `sameAs` entity links. Failed checks include
  fix guidance.

Both appear on the **AI Visibility** tab of the per-page drill-down.

## The per-page drill-down

Click any page (from **Pages** or **URL Checks**) to open its report at
`/{org}/{workspace}/audit/p/{pageId}`. The header shows the page's SEO and GEO
rings, URL, HTTP status, word count and issue count, plus an **Ask Spyro** button
and a **Re-check after fixes** button. Below that are five tabs:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Overview" icon="gauge">
    The page's SEO and GEO scores, HTTP status and word count, a severity summary
    (critical / warning / info counts) and the top issues on this page.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Issues - ?tab=issues" icon="circle-exclamation">
    Every issue on this page, grouped by type, each with its severity and fix text.
    A clean page shows a success state instead.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="AI Visibility - ?tab=ai-visibility" icon="robot">
    The page's GEO score with a status badge, the 16-point citation-readiness
    checklist, and the site-wide AI crawler access table.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Full Report - ?tab=full-report" icon="file-text">
    Every issue on this single page by severity, with how-to-fix guidance.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="History - ?tab=history" icon="trending-up">
    A before/after snapshot if you've re-checked the page, plus its SEO + GEO
    trend across past audits and URL checks.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

### Re-check after fixes

Made a change? Click **Re-check after fixes** on the page report. Spyro queues a
fresh check of just that URL - the button shows **Re-checking…** and the page
refreshes until it's done, then the **History** tab shows the before/after.

## Ask the audit assistant

The **Ask Spyro** button opens a chat assistant grounded in *your* audit. Ask
free-form questions - "why is my GEO score low?", "what should I fix first?" - and
it answers using your top issues, your domain and page URLs, and relevant content
pulled from your site. You can ask at the site level or scoped to a single page.

<Note>
Saving a conversation persists it and meters against your monthly AI credits;
unsaved chats are ephemeral. See [Plans, billing & usage](/product/billing-and-usage).
</Note>

## AI suggest fix

On an individual issue you can ask Spyro to **suggest a fix**. It streams back a
short, action-first recommendation (2-4 sentences, starting with a verb like
*Add*, *Set*, *Update* or *Remove*) tailored to your domain and the affected
pages - often with a small code snippet, e.g. `<h1>Your Page Title</h1>`. It's
advice to apply yourself; Spyro doesn't edit your site.

## Download the PDF report

Click **Export report to PDF** (enabled once the audit is complete) to download a
branded report. The filename follows `spyro-audit-{domain}-{date}.pdf`.

Modern audits export the full **V3 SEO + GEO report** - the same branded document
as the public SEO/GEO free tool, rendered with real Chromium for a pixel-perfect
result. (Audits run before the V3 report existed fall back to a lighter PDF.) See
[PDF Engine](/backend/pdf-engine) for how it's rendered.
