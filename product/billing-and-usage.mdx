Billing lives at the **organization** level, because what you pay for is governed
by your plan and how many sites you run — not by any single workspace. You'll find
it under **Plan & billing** (`/{org}/settings/billing`) and **Usage**
(`/{org}/usage`).

<Note>
Only org **owners and admins** can view billing, and only the **owner** can change
the plan or manage payment. Opening **Billing** inside a workspace redirects here.
</Note>

## The two plans

Both plans give every site the same capabilities — the difference is how many
sites you run.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Pro — $99/mo" icon="user">
    For founders growing **1–2 sites**. A second site adds another $99/mo (no
    discount). A third site moves you to Agency.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Agency — $99/site/mo" icon="users">
    For agencies running **3–20 sites**, with volume discounts that grow as you
    add sites. White-label included.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

### Agency volume discounts

The per-site price drops as your site count climbs:

| Sites | Discount | Effective per-site |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 3–5 | 10% off | ~$89/site/mo |
| 6–10 | 15% off | ~$84/site/mo |
| 11–20 | 20% off | ~$79/site/mo |
| 21+ | Custom | [Contact sales](mailto:hello@spyro.app) |

Running **21 or more sites** moves you to a custom plan — there's no self-serve
checkout for that tier; reach out at `hello@spyro.app`.

<Tip>
Pay **annually** and you pay for **10 months instead of 12** — roughly two months
free on either plan.
</Tip>

## The free trial

New organizations start with a **3-day trial** that lets you generate **3 blogs
total** (not 3 per month) so you can take the writer for a spin before you
subscribe. When the trial ends, you'll need an active subscription to keep
generating content.

## What's included

Every site on either plan gets the same monthly allowances:

| Capability | Allowance |
| --- | --- |
| Blog generations | **30 / month** |
| Spyro AI credits (chat + keyword research) | **200 / month** per workspace |
| Tracked rank-tracker keywords | **500** at once |
| Tracked prompts (AI visibility) | **10** |
| AI engines | All **5** — Google AI Overviews, ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude, Perplexity |
| Connected integrations | **5** per workspace |
| Blog ideas | 30 / month |
| Audit pages crawled | 500 |
| SERP lookups | 500 / month |
| In-content images per article | 3 |
| Competitor comparison | Included |
| White-label | **Agency only** |
| Team members | Unlimited on both plans |

The only real feature difference is that **white-label is Agency-only** — Agency
is effectively "Pro × N sites" with white-labeling on top.

## How usage is metered

Usage counts against your plan's monthly limits and **resets on the 1st of each
month** (UTC). The **Usage** page (`/{org}/usage`) shows total consumption across
*every* member of the org for the current period, with a progress bar per metric:

- **Spyro AI credits** — one shared pool per workspace, drawn down by both the AI
  chat and keyword research (listed per workspace).
- **SERP lookups**, **Audit pages crawled**, **Blog ideas**, and **Blog
  generations** — totaled across the org.

Unlimited metrics show "Unlimited on your plan" instead of a bar. The **Plan &
billing** page surfaces the headline numbers — current plan, blogs per month, and
workspace count.

<Note>
During the trial, **Blog generations** shows the 3-blog trial allowance rather
than the 30/month plan figure.
</Note>

## Checkout, the portal, and changing plans

Spyro bills through **Dodo Payments**. From **Plan & billing** (as the owner):

<Steps>
  <Step title="Subscribe">
    Pick a plan, interval (monthly or annual), and — for Agency — your site count
    in the plan selector. Spyro opens a **Dodo checkout** session for you to pay.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Change your plan">
    Already subscribed? Adjust the plan, interval, or Agency site count and Spyro
    updates your live subscription in place. You can't drop below the number of
    workspaces you currently have — remove some first.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Manage billing">
    Use **Manage billing** to open the **Dodo customer portal**, where you can
    update your card, download invoices, and see your subscription.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
If billing isn't connected yet (no Dodo configuration in the environment),
checkout is disabled and you'll see a notice on the billing page. See
[Billing](/backend/billing) for the engineering details.
</Warning>
