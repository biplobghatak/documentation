The **Writer** is where a single article comes together. You reach it by opening a
post from the [calendar](/product/calendar) (URL `/{org}/{workspace}/writer/{id}`).
It has two modes: a live **generation timeline** while the article is being
produced, and the **article workspace** once it's ready.

## While it generates

When you trigger an article, Spyro runs a pipeline — **research → draft → verify →
images** — and shows a live timeline. The page polls every few seconds and opens the
editor automatically once everything (including images) is done.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="What the timeline shows" icon="list-check">
    Each phase reports its status as it completes — researching and drafting,
    then "**Finishing up images…**" while the featured and in-content images are
    generated. The editor only opens once images are in the markdown.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="If it stalls or errors" icon="rotate">
    A **Retry** button appears for stuck or failed runs so you can re-trigger the
    pipeline without losing the post.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## The article workspace

Once the draft is ready, you get a full editing workspace:

- **Rich text editor** — a TipTap editor with slash commands, tables, images, and a
  selection bubble menu. Edit the body, headings, and inline formatting directly.
- **SEO** — the **meta title** and **meta description**, the target **keyword** with
  its volume and difficulty, and a **score panel** rating the draft.
- **Research** — the research pack and outline Spyro used, plus **quality checks**.
- **Images** — the brand-styled **featured image** and any **in-content images**,
  generated for you (see [Article settings → Image generation](/product/article-settings)).

## Publishing

<Steps>
  <Step title="Connect a destination">
    Add a WordPress, Shopify, or webhook integration in
    [Article settings → Integrations](/product/article-settings).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Publish or schedule">
    Push the finished article to a connected destination, or schedule it. The
    workspace shows the published URL and scheduled status.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Export">
    You can also export the draft (e.g. as Word or HTML) to take it elsewhere.
  </Step>
</Steps>
