Spyro is built for teams. You invite people by email, give them a role, and
decide which of your sites they can touch. Roles work on **two levels** — the
organization and the individual workspace — and understanding how they fit
together is the whole story.

## Two levels of access

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Organization roles" icon="building">
    Every member of an org has one **org role**: **owner**, **admin**,
    **manager**, or **member**. This sets their reach across *all* the org's
    workspaces. Manage them at **Members** under the org settings
    (`/{org}/settings/members`).
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Workspace roles" icon="folder">
    Within a single workspace, a person is either an **admin** or a **member**.
    Manage these on a workspace's **Team members** tab
    (`/{org}/{workspace}/settings/team-members`).
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## The role hierarchy

The key rule is which workspaces a person can reach:

| Org role | Reach | What they can do |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Owner** | Every workspace (admin) | Everything, plus billing, plan changes, and transferring ownership. |
| **Admin** | Every workspace (admin) | Manage members, settings, and content across all sites. |
| **Manager** | Every workspace (admin) | Admin on every workspace. |
| **Member** | Only assigned workspaces | Access the specific workspaces they've been added to, at the role they were given. |

In short: **owners, admins, and managers automatically have admin access to every
workspace** in the org. A plain **member sees only the workspaces they've been
explicitly assigned to** — and at the admin-or-member role set on each assignment.

<Note>
There is exactly one **owner** per organization. Only the owner can change the
plan, manage billing, or transfer ownership.
</Note>

## Inviting teammates

You can invite from either level, depending on the reach you want to grant.

### Invite to the organization

On **Members** (`/{org}/settings/members`), owners and admins enter an email and
pick an org role (**admin**, **manager**, or **member**), then send. Spyro emails
an invite link; you can also **Copy link** to share it yourself.

### Invite to a single workspace

On a workspace's **Team members** tab, a workspace admin enters an email and picks
**admin** or **member**. If that person is already in the org, they're added to
this workspace right away. If they're new, Spyro creates an invite that drops them
straight into *this* workspace once they accept.

<Tip>
Use a **workspace invite** to give someone access to just one site, and an
**organization invite** when you want them across everything (or as a manager who
reaches every workspace).
</Tip>

## Accepting an invite

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the link">
    The invitee clicks the link in their email (`/invite/{token}`).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Sign in with the invited address">
    If they don't have an account yet, they're sent to sign-up first. They must
    sign in with **the same email the invite was sent to** — a forwarded link
    can't be redeemed by a different account.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Land in the workspace">
    On acceptance they join the org at the invited role, get added to any
    workspaces the invite assigned, and are dropped into a workspace they can
    access. The person who invited them is notified.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
Invites expire after **14 days**. If a link has expired or been revoked, the
invitee sees an "Invite unavailable" message — just send a fresh one.
</Warning>

## Changing roles and removing people

- **Change an org role** — on **Members**, owners and admins pick a new role from
  the dropdown next to a member.
- **Remove from the org** — the trash icon removes a member from the organization
  entirely.
- **Remove from a workspace** — on **Team members**, the trash icon unassigns a
  member from just that workspace (they keep their org membership).
- **Revoke a pending invite** — use **Revoke** next to any unaccepted invite at
  either level.
- **Transfer ownership** — the owner can hand the org to another member with
  **Make owner**; the previous owner becomes an admin.

<Note>
The owner can't be removed or have their role changed. Only **owners and admins**
manage org members; only **workspace admins** manage a workspace's team.
</Note>
