Spyro keeps an eye on your competitors so you always know who you're up against and
where you can win. It tracks three kinds of competitor information: the **keywords**
they go after, the **topics** they cover that you don't, and how often **AI
assistants** mention them instead of you.

## Where to find competitor information

Competitor data shows up in a few places, depending on what you want to know.

### Your competitor list

Open **Settings** and go to **Competitors**. Here you'll see the competitors Spyro
has found for you - it discovers them automatically as it runs keyword and search
analysis - plus any you add yourself. Next to each one, you'll see how many
**keywords you share**, which is a quick sign of how directly you compete. You can
add or remove competitors at any time.

<Note>
The list shows a shared-keyword **count** (for example, "12 shared keywords"). To
see the actual keywords and a deeper breakdown, ask the AI agent (below).
</Note>

### Competitor keywords - ask the AI agent

For a closer look, just ask the [AI agent](/product/spyro-ai-agent) in plain English:

- **"Who are my competitors for [topic]?"** - Spyro runs a live Google search and
  lists the top sites ranking for that topic, with an estimate of how strong each
  one is.
- **"What keywords does [a competitor's website] target?"** - Spyro looks at that
  competitor's pages and pulls out the keywords they appear to be going after.

This shows what a competitor is *aiming* for, based on their own pages - a great way
to find ideas you might be missing.

### Content gaps become article ideas

When Spyro plans your content in the [content calendar](/product/calendar), it looks
at what your competitors cover that you don't and turns those gaps into blog topic
suggestions. So your competitor research quietly feeds your content plan.

### How often AI recommends them

On the [AI visibility](/product/ai-visibility) page, the **Competitors** ranking
shows which rival brands AI assistants mention for your buyer questions, how your
**share of voice** compares, and whether you're gaining or losing ground. The
[tracking prompts](/product/prompts) view even shows which competitor websites get
cited for each question.

## How to use it

<Steps>
  <Step title="Build your competitor list">
    Open **Settings → Competitors**. Add the rivals you care about, or let Spyro
    keep finding them for you.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Research their keywords">
    Ask the [AI agent](/product/spyro-ai-agent) who ranks for a topic, or what
    keywords a specific competitor targets.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check who AI recommends">
    Open [AI visibility](/product/ai-visibility) to see the competitor ranking and
    your share of voice.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Turn gaps into content">
    Let the [content calendar](/product/calendar) suggest topics from competitor
    gaps, then write and publish them with the [AI writer](/product/writer).
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What to expect

- The competitor list shows a shared-keyword **count**, not the full list - ask the
  agent when you want the details.
- Keyword research runs **on demand**, so you always get fresh results.
- Competitor AI citations need [tracking prompts](/product/prompts) first; they fill
  in after the next check (Spyro checks every 3 days).
- Your competitor list grows over time as Spyro learns your market.

## Tips

- Start with 3-5 close competitors so the comparisons stay meaningful.
- When a rival outranks you inside AI, open AI visibility and study the questions
  they win.
- Treat competitor content gaps as your next article ideas.
