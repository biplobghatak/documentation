**Settings** is where you tune a single workspace - its identity, the signals that
shape what Spyro writes, the pages it has read, and the keys other tools use to
reach it. Everything here is scoped to one website. You'll find it under
**Account → Settings** in the sidebar, at `/{org}/{workspace}/settings`.

<Note>
Opening **Settings** lands you on the **Account** tab
(`/{org}/{workspace}/settings/account`). The sub-tabs below run across the top of
the page.
</Note>

## Account

Your **Account** tab is a read-only summary of *you* in this workspace:

- **Email** - the address you signed in with. Spyro uses Google sign-in, so you
  manage the account itself (name, password, security) at
  [myaccount.google.com](https://myaccount.google.com/), not here.
- **Workspace access** - the workspace you're viewing, your role in it
  (**Admin** or **Member**), and the site's domain.

If you're an **Admin** you can manage this workspace's settings and team; if
you're a **Member**, ask a workspace admin to change your role.

## General

The **General** tab is the workspace's identity and lifecycle:

| Setting | What it does |
| --- | --- |
| **Workspace name** | The label shown in the switcher and across the app. |
| **Timezone** | Anchors schedules and reports - rank checks, citation checks, and the editorial calendar all read this. |
| **Re-crawl** | Re-reads your site so audits, scores, and the AI knowledge base reflect your latest pages. |
| **Tone & brand** | The voice and visual profile Spyro derived from your site, used when it writes. |
| **Business profile** | The business details Spyro keeps about this site. |

A status pill at the top shows whether the workspace is still **Crawling site**
or **Ready**. The site's **domain** is the fixed identity of the workspace and is
shown for reference.

<Warning>
The **Danger zone** at the bottom (workspace admins only) holds **Delete this
workspace**. It permanently removes the site and *all* of its audits, prompts,
ranks, posts, and citation history. There's no undo.
</Warning>

## Audience

The **Audience** tab (shown as **Audience & brand**) is two fields that directly
steer the [Writer](/product/writer) and [Article Planner](/product/calendar):

<Steps>
  <Step title="Audience segments">
    Short tags for the people you sell to - Spyro auto-derives these from your
    site during onboarding. Edit a tag inline, remove one with the `×`, or add
    your own. You can keep a handful of segments.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Business description">
    A paragraph describing what you do, who you serve, and what makes you
    distinctive. The more detail here, the more on-target Spyro's plans and drafts
    are.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
Treat these as living fields. Tightening your segments and description is the
single fastest way to improve the relevance of generated articles.
</Tip>

## Competitors

The **Competitors** tab manages the rival domains Spyro tracks for you. Spyro
seeds this list automatically (from shared-keyword data), and each entry shows
how many keywords you have in common. You can:

- **Add** a competitor domain you want tracked.
- **Remove** any you don't care about.

Confirmed competitors feed your [AI visibility](/product/ai-visibility)
share-of-voice comparison and the [rank tracker](/product/rank-tracker), so keep
the list to the rivals that actually matter.

## Crawled pages

The **Crawled pages** tab (shown as **Knowledge base**) is the inventory of every
page Spyro found during your last site crawl. For each page you see its URL,
template type, **SEO** and **GEO** scores, HTTP status, word count, and worst
issue severity - click a row to open that page's audit report.

At the top, an **AI knowledge base** bar shows how much of your site Spyro has
read and saved into its memory (the index that powers
[Spyro AI research](/product/spyro-ai-agent) and the writer).

### Add pages manually

Spyro doesn't always discover every URL. Use **Add pages manually** to paste in
URLs (one per line, up to 10 at a time). Each is crawled, scored, and added to
your search index immediately. URLs off this site's domain, or pages blocked by
`robots.txt`, are skipped and reported back to you.

<Note>
If you've never run an audit, this tab will prompt you to run your first
[site audit](/product/audit) - that's the crawl that fills the page list.
</Note>

## API

The **API** tab issues the keys that authenticate the public Spyro API
(`/api/v1`) for this workspace. Every key starts with the `sk_ws_` prefix and is
scoped to this one workspace.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create a key">
    Give the key a name so you can tell your integrations apart. You can hold up
    to **3 keys** per workspace at once.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Use it">
    Send the key as a bearer token on your API requests. You can reveal a key's
    full value anytime from this tab.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Revoke it">
    Delete a key to revoke it instantly. Anything still using that key stops
    working.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
Treat each key like a password - anyone holding it can act on this workspace. Only
workspace **admins** can create or delete keys.
</Warning>

For request formats, endpoints, and examples, see the
[public API reference](/backend/public-api).

## The other settings entries

A few items that look like settings are really shortcuts into the **Article
Planner**. Opening **Blog**, **Article defaults**, **Text generation**, **Image
generation**, or **Integrations** under Settings redirects you to the matching
Article Planner screen, where those defaults actually live. See
[Article settings](/product/article-settings).

<Note>
**Billing** lives at the *organization* level, not per workspace - opening it here
redirects to [Plans, billing & usage](/product/billing-and-usage).
</Note>
