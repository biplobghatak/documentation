Connecting **Google Search Console (GSC)** lets Spyro work from your *real* search
data instead of estimates. Once linked, you get query and page performance, trend
charts, AI-surface impressions, and a set of **diagnoses** that pair GSC signals
with your latest crawl and hand you concrete fix plans. You'll find it under
**SEO → Search Console** at `/{org}/{workspace}/gsc`.

<Note>
This is the **product guide** - how to use the screen. For how the OAuth flow,
import jobs and diagnoses are built, see [Integrations](/backend/integrations) in
the developer docs.
</Note>

## Connect Google Search Console

<Steps>
  <Step title="Start the connection">
    On the Search Console screen, click **Connect Google Search Console**. Spyro
    redirects you to Google's sign-in and consent screen.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Grant read-only access">
    Spyro requests a single **read-only** scope
    (`webmasters.readonly`). As the screen says: *"Read-only - Spyro can't change
    anything in your GSC account."* It only reads impressions, clicks, CTR,
    position and your sitemap inventory.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Pick a verified property">
    After consent you land on **Pick a verified property**. Spyro lists every
    property your Google account can access - signed in as your Google email.
    Choose the one whose data should power this workspace and confirm.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Wait for the first import">
    Selecting a property queues a performance import. The header shows
    **Performance import queued - refresh in a minute or two**, then
    **Last imported: …** once data lands.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
If the property's domain doesn't match your workspace domain, Spyro flags it:
*"This GSC property doesn't match the workspace domain - diagnoses may not line up
with your crawl."* You can still proceed, but for accurate diagnoses pick the
property that matches the site you're tracking.
</Warning>

A property is either a **URL-prefix** site (like `https://example.com/`) or a
**Domain** property (`sc-domain:example.com`) - Spyro supports both. To change
later, use **Switch property**; to remove the link entirely, use **Disconnect**,
which revokes the token at Google and deletes the stored connection.

## What you'll see once connected

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Overview (last 28 days)" icon="chart-simple">
    Four headline stats for the last 28 days - **Clicks**, **Impressions**,
    **CTR** and **Average position** - over the dated window.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Trend charts" icon="chart-line">
    A daily time series of clicks, impressions, CTR and average position going back
    up to ~16 months, with a range slider and metric toggle.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Top pages and queries" icon="table">
    Your **top pages** and **top queries** by clicks in the latest 28-day window,
    each showing clicks, impressions, CTR and position.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="AI surfaces" icon="robot">
    Where supported, impressions from Google's generative surfaces - **AI
    Overviews**, **AI Mode** and **AI in Discover** - as a daily chart plus the top
    pages by AI impressions. Google exposes this only in its UI today, so Spyro
    tracks it on a best-effort basis.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Diagnoses and fix plans

Once the first import has a few days of data, Spyro surfaces **diagnosis cards**.
Each card pairs a GSC signal with the matching finding from your latest
[site audit](/product/audit) and a step-by-step **fix plan**. The signals Spyro
looks for:

| Diagnosis | What it catches |
| --- | --- |
| **Low CTR** | High-impression pages with an unusually low click-through rate - usually a title/meta-description rewrite. |
| **Almost ranking** | Pages sitting at positions ~8–20 with real impressions - a nudge of content and internal links could push them to page one. |
| **Content decay** | Pages whose clicks dropped sharply versus the previous window - refresh the content and date. |
| **Submitted but noindex** | A page in your sitemap that returns `noindex` - remove the noindex or drop it from the sitemap. |
| **Canonical mismatch** | A page canonicalised elsewhere - fix the canonical or redirect. |
| **Missing in GSC** | A crawled page with no impressions in 90 days - get it indexed (sitemap, internal links, request indexing). |

Each fix plan ends with the same reminder: **validate the fix in Google Search
Console after applying it.** Spyro only *reads* GSC - it never changes anything in
your Google account, so you confirm the result there yourself. When your site looks
clean, you'll see a healthy state instead: *"Your site looks healthy against the
signals Spyro can see… We'll check again on Monday."*

<Note>
**GA4 / Analytics is built but currently hidden from the nav.** The Analytics item

</Note>
