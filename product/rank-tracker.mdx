The **Rank tracker** shows where your pages land in Google for the keywords you
care about, and charts how those positions move week to week. You add the
keywords you want to watch, and Spyro keeps an eye on them for you - so you always
know whether you're climbing or slipping.

## Why use it

Rankings change all the time, and it's easy to miss it when a key page drops.
The rank tracker keeps your important keywords in one place, flags the biggest
movers, and shows your progress as a simple chart - so you can see what's working
and react quickly when something slides.

## How to use it

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Rank tracker">
    Go to **Rank tracker** in the left sidebar.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add a keyword">
    Type a keyword into the **Track keyword** box and submit. Spyro checks it
    right away, so the position appears within seconds.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Watch it over time">
    Each keyword becomes a row showing its current position, its best-ever
    position, and how it has moved - with a mini chart of its recent history.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
A counter at the top shows how many keyword slots you've used (for example,
`120/500`). It turns amber as you get close to the limit.
</Tip>

## What the table shows

Each keyword you track gets its own row, with:

- **The keyword** and the page that's ranking for it
- **Current position** - where you sit in Google right now (or "not in top 100")
- **Best** - the best position you've ever reached
- **Change** - which way it moved since the last check (green up, red down)
- **History** - a small chart of recent checks, so you can spot trends at a glance

You can sort, search, and filter the list - including by whether you added a
keyword yourself or Spyro added it automatically.

## How often positions are checked

- **New keywords are checked right away.** As soon as you add one, Spyro checks
  its position so you don't have to wait.
- **Existing keywords are re-checked every week.** Spyro updates all your tracked
  keywords once a week, and shows the next update time on the page.

## Automatic keyword tracking

Here's the part you don't have to lift a finger for. **Every time you publish an
article, Spyro automatically starts tracking the best keyword for it** - and it's
smart about which one to pick.

Instead of just grabbing the obvious keyword, Spyro chooses the one that article
is most likely to actually rank for, based on how competitive the keyword is and
how strong your site is:

- On a **strong, established site**, it usually tracks the article's main keyword
  - you can realistically rank for it.
- On a **newer site**, it tracks an easier, more specific keyword first. As your
  site grows stronger, the bigger keyword becomes worth tracking later.

The keyword it picks gets an **Auto** badge that links back to the article, and
it's checked right away so you see its starting position immediately.

## Good to know

- You can track up to **500 keywords** per website - both the ones you add and the
  ones Spyro adds automatically.
- **Deleting a keyword frees a slot right away**, so prune ones you no longer need
  to make room for new ones.
- If you're at the limit, Spyro lets you know when you try to add another, and it
  quietly skips auto-tracking until you free up space.
