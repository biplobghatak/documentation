The **Article Planner** is where you turn ideas into a publishing schedule. It pairs
an **editorial calendar** (every planned post, laid out by day) with a **Plan with
Spyro** panel that researches and schedules topics for you. It's at
`/{org}/{workspace}/calendar` (**Article Planner → Calendar** in the sidebar).

<Note>
Spyro researches and schedules a starter set of topics **automatically after
onboarding**, so your calendar usually isn't empty on day one.
</Note>

## The calendar board

The board shows one month at a time. Each scheduled post appears on its day with a
status:

| Status | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| **Scheduled** | Planned for that day, not yet generated. |
| **Queued / Generating** | The writer is producing the article (the board auto-refreshes as it progresses). |
| **Done** | Draft is ready — click to open it in the [Writer](/product/writer). |

Use the **‹ ›** arrows to move between months. While any post is generating, the
board polls and refreshes on its own so finished articles appear without a manual
reload.

## Plan with Spyro

The **Plan with Spyro** panel (right side) is a chat that builds your plan — it
shares the same conversation threads as the [Spyro AI agent](/product/spyro-ai-agent).
Ask it to propose topics, then approve and schedule them. **Generate more topics**
runs a fresh research pass to extend the plan.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Generate or request topics">
    Let onboarding seed them, click **Generate more topics**, or ask the planner
    chat for specific angles.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Schedule them">
    Approved ideas drop onto the calendar by day; drag to reschedule.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Send to the writer">
    Open a scheduled idea and generate the article — it flows into the
    [Writer](/product/writer).
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Your blog quota

The header shows **Blogs left this month** (or **Blogs left in trial**) — your
monthly article allowance and how many remain. The allowance is set by your plan
(30 blogs/month on Pro and Agency; 3 during the trial). See
[Plans, billing & usage](/product/billing-and-usage).

<Warning>
Without an LLM key configured, generation produces a **placeholder draft** (no
words counted) so you can see the flow. Add the key for real articles.
</Warning>
