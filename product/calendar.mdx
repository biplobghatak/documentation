The **content calendar** is where you plan your blog. Instead of staring at a blank
page, you get a month-by-month board of topic ideas - each one placed on a day -
so it's easy to see what's coming up and stay consistent.

The best part: Spyro does the research for you. It studies your website and your
market, then fills your calendar with topic ideas worth writing about. You stay in
charge - you decide what to keep, when to publish it, and when to send an idea to
the writer.

## Why use it

- **Never run out of ideas.** Spyro suggests topics based on your site and your
  competitors.
- **Stay consistent.** A clear monthly plan helps you publish regularly, which is
  what search engines and AI assistants reward.
- **Save time.** The research and scheduling are done for you - you just review and
  approve.

## How to use it

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the calendar">
    Click **Calendar** in the sidebar. After you set up your account, Spyro has
    usually already added a starter set of topic ideas, so your calendar won't be
    empty on day one.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Ask Spyro to plan more topics">
    Use the **Plan with Spyro** panel to ask for more ideas - for example, a
    specific angle or theme you want to cover. Spyro researches fresh topics and
    adds them to your plan.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Schedule or reschedule by day">
    Each idea sits on a day. To move one, drag it to a different date. This lets you
    space out your posts however you like.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Send an idea to the writer">
    When you're ready to publish, open a scheduled idea and start writing. Spyro
    turns it into a full article in the [AI writer](/product/writer).
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What to expect

The calendar shows one month at a time - use the arrows to move between months.
Each idea shows a simple status: **scheduled** (planned but not written yet),
**writing** (Spyro is creating the article right now), and **done** (the draft is
ready to open in the writer). When an article is being written, the board updates on
its own, so you don't need to refresh.

Near the top you'll see how many articles you have left this month. Your plan sets
this monthly allowance - see [Plans, billing & usage](/product/billing-and-usage)
for the details.

## Tips

- Let Spyro seed the first batch of ideas, then ask **Plan with Spyro** for more
  whenever you want to fill out the month.
- Keep a steady rhythm - a few well-spaced posts each week usually beats a big batch
  all at once.
- Set your tone, style, and images first in [Content settings](/product/article-settings)
  so every article matches your brand.
